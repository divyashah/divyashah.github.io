Divya Shah

PhD Fellow in Robot Design

Italian Institute of Technology

University of Genoa

Divya Shah received his B. Tech degree in mechanical engineering from Sardar Patel College of Engineering affiliated to the Mumbai University, India in 2015. His active involvement with the collegiate robotics club during his undergrad, aspired him to pursue higher education in robotics which brought him to Europe.

He then, graduated from the Erasmus+ European Masters on Advanced Robotics (EMARO+) with double M.Sc. degrees from the Università degli Studi di Genova, Italy and École Centrale de Nantes, France in 2017. For his graduate thesis, he worked on CAD design and optimization of a redundant robotics workcell for automated fiber placement process.

Currently, he is a PhD student with iCub Tech at the Istituto Italiano di Tecnologia (IIT) and the Dipartimento di Informatica, Bioingegneria, Robtoica e Ingegneria dei Sistemi (DIBRIS) at Università degli Studi di Genova, Italy. His research focuses on mechanism design, kinematics, parallel robots and tendon-driven mechanisms aiming for dexterous manipulation for humanoids.
